Hi! I'm a computer science major at Yale University, with a strong side interest in cognitive science.

I've been on a bit of a user-centred design/user experience design kick recently; apart from that, I illustrate, code, write, draw comics, spend time moseying around the internet, sing, etc.

I blog about UX and cooking and language and miscellany on Jess in Mannheim. In my free time I also do a lot of art, which you can view at my portfolio.

Academic interests I hope to study in depth one day (these are mostly very vague, I know): Human computer interfaces, unobtrusive ways of introducing multisensory input, and whether such interfaces affect cognitive gains. How body movement (proprioception?) and sensory input affect the encoding and processing of information in the brain. High-level effects of computing/robotics on social behavior, social cognition and mental health; investigating the presence of brain-level differences between information processing performed online and off. Computing as it relates to interpersonal relationships, emotional dysregulation, attachment and cognitive style, mood/personality/anxiety and autism spectrum disorders; Therapeutic uses of computers/robots for enhancing general well-being and treatment of disorders; Multicultural user interface and user experience design; Impact of culture on behavior and cognition.
