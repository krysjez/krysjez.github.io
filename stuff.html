Art

  • I like illustration and comics, in case you couldn't tell. I'm also fond of, but less good at, calligraphy and large outdoor installations/public art.
  • Favorite artists: James Gurney, Olafur Eliasson, Alexander McQueen, Donato Giancola, Petar Meseldžija, Vincent van Gogh, Mona Hatoum, Phobs, Paul Duffield, Shilin.

Travel/Favorite Cities

  • My travel style usually involves visiting museums, parks, and a lot of walking/cycling and city exploration.
  • Singapore: I say this as unbiasedly as I can be, after having been to many places.
  • Aarhus, Denmark: a charming city that comes with many of my favorite things - bicycles, nice parks, a museum with at least two great Olafur Eliasson pieces, and the sea: I'm in love. (I've only been there in the summer, though. I'd very much like to live there for a little longer.)
  • Pittenweem, Scotland: not a city; a tiny, tiny village really. It's very beautiful in the summer and has a sweet shop that sells the most delicious tablet. Also on the sea, which adds 100 points in my book.
  • New York City, USA: a very different kind of city from Singapore, and the closest I can feel to home in this corner of the States. Manhattan is on the sea, I guess.
  • Taipei, Taiwan: an interesting blend of old and new; it felt a little like the Singapore of my early childhood. Also on an island! I think we're starting to see a trend here.
  • Hangzhou/Suzhou, China: a rarity: a mainland Chinese city I feel at ease in.

Books and Comics

  • I keep track of reading on my Goodreads profile.
  • Favorite fiction: Most of the Tolkien legendarium, The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss, Brandon Sanderson's stuff, Phillip Pullman's His Dark Materials. Yes, my favorite genre is high fantasy. I also like Haruki Murakami's writing style.
  • Favorite comic EVER: The Order of the Stick. Smart humor, occassional geeky references and great character development. The first 100+ strips aren't that great, but it gets good quickly after that.
